Accessibility Rights For PWDS

Republic Act No. 7277 (Magna Carta for PWDs)

  • Promotes the rights and welfare of PWDs, ensuring they enjoy the same opportunities as others.
  • Requires removal of barriers and provision of access facilities in public spaces, transportation, and digital communication.
  • Covers areas like employment, education, health, accessibility, telecommunications, and social services.

Republic Act No. 11106 (Filipino Sign Language Act)

  • Ensures equal access to information by requiring Filipino Sign Language (FSL) in broadcast media and online content
  • Requires the use of FSL in media, online platforms, education, and government services to ensure equal access to information and participation in society.
  • Promotes inclusive communication and digital accessibility by mandating training, materials, and programs that integrate FSL in both physical and digital spaces.

DICT Memorandum Circular (MC) No. 004

  • Adopted the W3C Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.0 as the national standard for web content.
  • Make government websites and online services accessible to a wider range of people with disabilities.
  • All national government agencies were required to comply.

Case Study

Why PhilHealth?

PhilHealth, as a vital healthcare provider, must ensure its website is accessible since any barriers could prevent PWDs from accessing benefits, information, and essential health services.

Accessibility Issues

Visual Disabilities (Blind/Low Vision)

-Many images lack proper alt text, making it hard for screen readers to describe content.

-Text contrast is sometimes low (e.g., gray text on white background), reducing readability for people with low vision.

Motor Disabilities

-Small clickable elements (tiny buttons, links close together) make it difficult for users with limited motor control to navigate.

-No full keyboard navigation‚Äîsome forms and menus require mouse input.

Cognitive Disabilities

-Information-heavy text with no simplified ‚Äúeasy-to-read‚Äù option can overwhelm users with learning or cognitive difficulties.

-Inconsistent layout and navigation can confuse users trying to find specific information.

"What if a group of visually impaired PWDs decided to file a class-action lawsuit against a prominent government agency, arguing that its newly launched public services portal is completely unusable with a screen reader?"

Possible Action To Be Taken

The group, with the support of National Council on Disability Affairs (NCDA), files a class-action lawsuit against the agency. Their legal claim is based on:

  • Republic Act 7277 (Magna Carta for Persons with Disabilities), which guarantees accessibility and equal opportunity.
  • Republic Act 11106 (Filipino Sign Language Act) and related digital accessibility policies, emphasizing inclusive communication.

Possible Outcomes

Legal Victory for PWDs

- Court mandates the agency to redesign the portal for accessibility (WCAG compliance).

- Sets a precedent, pushing other agencies to review their digital platforms.

- Strengthens advocacy for inclusive ICT policies.

Settlement / Mediation

- The agency avoids trial by committing to immediate accessibility fixes.

- Partnership formed with PWD groups for continuous accessibility testing.

DEVELOPER'S CHECKLIST

SCREEN READER SUPPORT
Ensure your content works seamlessly with assistive technologies for visually impaired users.
CAPTION VIDEOS
Provide accurate captions and transcripts for all audio and video content.
RESIZABLE TEXT
Allow text to be resized up to 200% without loss of functionality or content.
AVOID CAPTCHA PUZZLES
Use simple, accessible verification methods instead of complex visual puzzles.
AVOID FLASHING CONTENT
Prevent seizures by avoiding rapidly flashing or strobing visual content.
KEYBOARD NAVIGATION
Ensure all interactive elements are accessible via keyboard without a mouse.
